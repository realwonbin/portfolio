I had climbed this hill several times in the past, but only now did I come face to face with the statue of General MacArthur. Alongside people without any particular agenda, I wandered with my back to the sea.

He said he had lived in this neighborhood all his life. He told me where to go, and we parted. But soon after, from a distance, he called out to me again. Perhaps he found me unreliable. This time, he led me directly to the location of a drama that had aired recently.

He said he recognized it instantlyâ€” a small alley inside an old building, with an oddly foreign structure, a place where he had played with friends since childhood. He showed a brief excitement.

I did not know the drama, and the conversation did not flow. So I stepped away, as naturally as I could.
