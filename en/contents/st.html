《Stone Tower》 / 2015


A moment of completeness arises when weight, balance, texture, and scale interact organically.
Nothing can be added or removed. It is irreducible—without alternatives. And yet, completeness is not singular.
It endlessly emerges through recomposition. Each moment is final and irreversible, yet infinitely reachable.

The stone tower rises with time and seals itself when no more stones can be placed.
When encountered rarely in a mountain path or by a river, its origin or age remains unknowable regardless of height.
Some towers seem caught in the flow of time, awaiting just one more stone. If they collapse and are rebuilt, regret lingers at your fingertips—
like shattering a glass, they break and scatter anew.

When the whetstone is set, the tower escapes time. It becomes an event.
Each stone is entangled with others—no hierarchy, no sequence.
Causality dissolves, direction disappears, and judgment loses meaning.
It is a living thing—wholly independent and organically present.

The integrity of such moments is not fundamentally different from randomness.
One single moment, one person, one universe—infinitely unfolded.
