Renmin Road / 2008

Shanghai is a city of stark contrasts. Walking along Nanjing East Road, glancing into the alleys between the high-rises, the shadows felt deep. Development logic dominates here too. I remembered a Chinese proverb from a travel book: “The old must go for the new to come.” It made sense in the context of explaining the city’s economic center—but somehow, evaluating the old purely by economic value didn’t sit well.
I sought out older markets relatively untouched by development. One such market lies between the bustling People’s Square and the popular Old Shanghai Street. It is already half-demolished, with debris scattered across the site. It won’t be here much longer. Continuing from my previous work on the Hwanghak-dong market in Seoul, I documented hand-painted signage in this district of Shanghai.
